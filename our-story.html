Our Story

Chapter One — 1939

A Small Workshop, a Simple Belief

In the spring of 1939, Harold Foogle and Margaret Carrey rented a small workshop on the outskirts of a modest industrial town known primarily for its grain silos and a high density of teeth. Harold was a chemist. Margaret was a bookkeeper with a knack for delivering value. Together, they had one idea: toothpaste.

For the next sixty years, Foogle & Carrey made toothpaste. Harold perfected the formulations, while Margaret made every significant business decision. In 1962, they rebranded as Carrefoogle, each designing their half of the logo. One thing led to another, and by the end of the 1990s, it was one of the largest toothpaste manufacturers in the world.

Harold and Margaret, 1962. Visual representation. May not reflect actual founders.

Chapter Two — 1999

Breakthrough with the Bluetooth Toothpaste™

By 1999, Harold Foogle was ninety-one years old, and he could no longer hold a toothbrush. The company that had spent six decades perfecting oral hygiene was confronted with a simple problem: its co-founder couldn’t brush his own teeth.

The result was Bluetooth Toothpaste™: a proprietary paste that, once applied to the mouth, could be activated and controlled wirelessly via a paired device. In 1999, this meant a dedicated handheld unit roughly the size of a television remote, featuring a single button and an LED that blinked green when brushing was “complete.” It was the moment the company understood that its real purpose had never been toothpaste: it was making life easier.

Today, Bluetooth Toothpaste™ pairs seamlessly with the Carrefoogle companion app (iOS, Android). Brushing sessions are logged, scored, and shared with your dental provider automatically.

Chapter Three — 2000–Present

From Toothpaste to Everything

Carrefoogle Global Headquarters.

Bluetooth Toothpaste™ attracted attention, then capital, then acquisitions that moved faster than anyone was allowed to document. Today, Carrefoogle Corporation spans consumer technology, food, health, energy, yogurt, finance, media, water, and air — you're likely using our products as we speak. In 2027, Carrefoogle will introduce Bestie 3.0 — the most advanced companion AI ever developed.

Somewhere along the way, the company outgrew its founders. Harold and Margaret, by then honorary brand ambassadors, passed within a decade of each other. At Carrefoogle, no single individual is responsible for any one decision. Leadership changes. The mission doesn’t. Their portraits hang in every lobby worldwide — always elderly, always kind, always together.

“Margaret used to say: ‘The strongest relationships are the ones where nobody needs anything from the other person.’ We carry that with us every day.”
The Carrefoogle Board of Stewardship
