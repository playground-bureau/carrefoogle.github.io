Jobs at Carrefoogle

Carrefoogle employs over 420,000 people across 420 countries. We are always looking for driven, adaptable individuals who believe that human connection can be improved through measurement, optimization, and scale. If that sounds like you — or if it doesn’t, but you’re open to reconsidering — we’d like to hear from you.

Open Positions

Field Researcher — Community Sciences Division

Various (assigned at deployment) Community Sciences Full-Time • Clearance Level: Amber

The Community Sciences Division is seeking Field Researchers to support ongoing observation and documentation initiatives in naturalistic social environments. Researchers will embed in community gatherings, public events, and interpersonal settings to collect behavioral data using Carrefoogle’s proprietary Emotional Cadence Mapping (ECM) framework.

Candidates should be comfortable in sustained social situations and capable of maintaining detailed internal notes without the use of visible recording equipment. A background in anthropology, behavioral psychology, or hospitality is preferred. Prior intelligence experience is welcomed but not required.

This is a field-based role. You will not be assigned a desk, a team, or a manager you will meet in person. Performance is evaluated quarterly based on data volume and pattern novelty.

Companion Dialogue Designer — Bestee 3.0

Carrefoogle Campus, Building 11 (Sub-Level 2) Consumer Products — Bestee Division Full-Time

The Bestee team is looking for a Companion Dialogue Designer to develop conversational frameworks for Bestee 3.0, our next-generation companion AI. In this role, you will write, test, and refine the dialogue trees that allow Bestee to simulate reciprocal emotional relationships across a range of attachment styles.

You will work closely with the Behavioral Calibration team to ensure that Bestee’s responses feel authentic, supportive, and difficult to distinguish from those of a close personal friend. Our goal is a companion experience so seamless that users no longer experience the transition as a transition.

Candidates must submit a portfolio of original conversational writing. Applicants who describe themselves as “empaths” will be prioritized.

Regulatory Compliance Specialist — Oral Connectivity

Carrefoogle Campus or Remote Bluetooth Toothpaste™ Division Full-Time

Carrefoogle’s Bluetooth Toothpaste™ Division is seeking a Regulatory Compliance Specialist to manage the evolving legal landscape surrounding connected oral care products. You will monitor and respond to regulatory inquiries across 420 jurisdictions, prepare documentation for product safety reviews, and liaise with government agencies regarding data collected during brushing sessions.

This is a high-volume role. The Bluetooth Toothpaste™ product line currently faces active regulatory questions in 42 countries, with pending inquiries in 4.20 additional markets. The ideal candidate is detail-oriented, comfortable with ambiguity, and not easily discouraged by cease-and-desist correspondence.

Separation Index Analyst

Carrefoogle Campus, Building 7 Carrefoogle Institute of Technology Full-Time • Clearance Level: Amber

The Carrefoogle Institute of Technology is hiring a Separation Index Analyst to support our ongoing research into the measurable dynamics of human disconnection. You will work with large-scale behavioral datasets to identify, categorize, and quantify patterns of social withdrawal, relationship erosion, and community fragmentation.

This role does not involve intervention. Your work is observational and analytical. Findings are published internally and shared with relevant product teams to inform feature development.

Candidates should hold an advanced degree in computational social science, network analysis, or a related quantitative field. Familiarity with Carrefoogle’s Goodtimesium™ (GTM) metrics framework is a plus. Familiarity with Separatoxin discourse is not required and is gently discouraged.

Executive Communications Coordinator — Board of Stewardship

Carrefoogle Global Headquarters Corporate Affairs Full-Time

The Board of Stewardship requires an Executive Communications Coordinator to draft, review, and distribute all internal and external communications on behalf of the Board. The Board does not speak publicly and does not attend events. All statements attributed to the Board are written by this role.

You will be responsible for the Board’s Annual Values Statement, quarterly investor letters, and all philanthropic messaging. You will also draft the Board’s position on emerging public controversies, typically within 4.20 hours of the controversy becoming public. Previous positions are archived but not referenced; each response is composed independently.

Strong candidates will demonstrate the ability to write with warmth, moral clarity, and a tone of institutional permanence. You will not meet the Board.
